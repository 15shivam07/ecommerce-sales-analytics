# Key Findings

## Executive Summary

The Superstore business generated approximately $2.26M in revenue from 4,922 orders across 793 unique customers.

---

## Customer Concentration Findings

| Metric | Result |
|----------|----------|
| Top 10% Customers | 31.16% Revenue |
| Top 20% Customers | 48.48% Revenue |
| Top 50% Customers | 80.43% Revenue |

### Observation

Revenue is moderately concentrated among a relatively small group of customers.

### Business Implication

Retaining high-value customers is critical while expanding the broader customer base to reduce concentration risk.

---

## Geographic Findings

### Top Revenue States

1. California
2. New York
3. Texas
4. Washington
5. Pennsylvania

### Observation

The top five states contribute approximately 51.85% of total revenue.

### Business Implication

Revenue dependence on a small number of states creates both opportunity and risk.

---

## Product Findings

### Best Performing Category

Technology

### Top Technology Sub-Categories

- Phones
- Machines
- Accessories

### Observation

Technology consistently outperforms Furniture and Office Supplies across regions.

### Business Implication

Technology products should remain a primary growth focus.

---

## Revenue Trend Findings

### Observation

Revenue growth accelerated significantly from 2016 onward, with stronger performance during Q4 months.

### Business Implication

Seasonality should be considered when planning inventory and marketing campaigns.

---

## Final Conclusion

The business demonstrates strong growth potential but exhibits moderate customer and geographic concentration. Expanding customer diversification and leveraging high-performing technology products can improve long-term revenue stability.
